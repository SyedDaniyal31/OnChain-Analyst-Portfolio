##  Case Studies & Research

###  ETFs bought 377K BTC and MSTR bought 371K. Before July 2025.
**Date:** [July 2025]  
**Tools Used:** CryptoQuant.
**Objective:** Track BTC ETFs Buying to predict potential price volatility.  
**Process:**
- This Chart Show data before July 2025. Despite continued Bitcoin accumulation by ETFs, Strategy (MSTR), and other corporate treasuries, analysts are questioning why the price hasn't surged to new all-time highs. One key reason is that these institutional purchases, while substantial, represent only a fraction of total spot market demand. For example, at the peak of Bitcoin demand growth on December 7 2024, ETFs and MSTR’s Bitcoin demand growth represented 33% of total Bitcoin demand growth–771K Bitcoin–, which underscores that there is a bigger and unobservable demand for Bitcoin Moreover, broader spot demand has been weakening in recent months. Overall demand for Bitcoin contracted significantly, declining by 895K Bitcoin in the last thirty days, effectively offsetting the positive price impact of institutional buying. As a result, even though headline purchases continue, the net demand growth for Bitcoin is slowing, limiting upward price momentum and keeping the asset in a consolidation phase rather than fueling a breakout. Additionally, Bitcoin purchases from US-based ETFs and Strategy (MSTR) have decelerated so far this year compared to the November-December 2024 period. Bitcoin purchases made by ETFs in a 30-day period have declined from 86K Bitcoin in December 7 2024, to 71K Bitcoin in May 18 2025, and to 40K Bitcoin today, a 53% decline in this period. Meanwhile, MSTR’s purchases have declined to 16K Bitcoin in the last thirty days, after peaking at 171K Bitcoin in December 7 2024, a 90% decline. The bottom line is that ETFs and MSTR’s Bitcoin purchases, while overall positive for Bitcoin price gains, are not sufficient to drive prices to fresh all-time highs.
